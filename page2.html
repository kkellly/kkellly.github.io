STD Awareness Campaign For Young Women

Year | 2020

This campaign aims to reduce the stigma around STD among young women,
educating and encouraging early testing.

The campaign is done through a concept shop and on Instagram, as follows:
When: Valentine’s Day
Where: Popular spots for young couples —
in front of bars, restaurants & campuses

1.
The Logo

Considering how flowers and petals have a similar shape to the bacteria, the campaign applies
flowers as the metaphor to the infection. With the positive connotations
flowers are usually associated with, they are a more approachable and easier way to
look into STD, which then helps reduce the stigma around the infection.

2.
Graphics & In-Store Stickers/Cards

Simple infographics presenting information about the 5 major types of STDs.
Stickers and cards are also distributed for free, containing Infection information, Clinic addresses (Hong Kong Island, Kowloon,
New Territories), Testing Processes.

3.
The Concept Shop

The visitors' path is planned according to the following principle:

1

Attract

2

Engage

3

Commit

Step 1: Attract —
Educational Videos &
3D Models

3D models of flowers imitating structures of the bacteria, with labelling and explanation on LED Screen.






Step 2a: Engage —
Q&A Wall

Visitors are welcomed to write down questions related to STDs anonymously, making it easier to share and open up.

The questions will be answered on Instagram, enhancing the engagement between the campaign and the visitors.



Step 2b: Engage —
Customizable Condom Packaging Machine

Interactive, customizable
and free — Encouraging the use of condoms. 






Step 3: Commit —
Quick In-Shop Testing Service & Referral

Providing the simple and
quick “cheek swab” test, the service enhances the visitors’ accessibility to STD testing services, encouraging early testing.

If necessary, further examination can be done with the clinics listed on the cards.
